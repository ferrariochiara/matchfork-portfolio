About Laura:
Laura is a digital marketing professional who manages campaigns and coordinates teams. In her free time, she enjoys going out with friends and exploring new restaurants, though she often struggles to decide due to the group's diverse preferences. She's organized, practical, and seeks quick, efficient solutions to simplify her day-to-day life.

Frustrations

  • Endless discussions with friends about where to eat
  • Lack of a tool that centralizes group preferences
  • Existing apps are unintuitive or have excessive advertising

Skills

  • Expert at managing mobile applications
  • Good organizer and natural leader in her friend group
  • Tech-savvy and quick to adopt new tools

Goals

  • Simplify group decision-making about where to eat
  • Discover new restaurants that fit her group's preferences
  • Save time and avoid arguments when organizing outings

Motivations

  • Enjoy stress-free dining experiences
  • Find tools that balance planning with her limited time
  • Make everyone in the group happy with the choice

Understanding Our Users

Both personas represent key user segments for MatchFork, each bringing unique perspectives and needs to the platform

Proto Persona - Laura

Role in Development: Laura represents our initial hypothesis about our target user. As a working professional with limited time, she needs efficiency and simplicity. Her proto persona helped us establish core features like group synchronization and the swipe system during early design phases.

Key Insight: Laura showed us that our users are natural group organizers who need tools that respect their time while ensuring everyone has a voice in the decision.

User Persona - Camila

Role in Development: Camila emerged from actual user research, validating and refining our assumptions. As a student with anxiety about disappointing others, she highlighted the need for detailed restaurant information, reviews, and photos to reduce decision-making stress.

Key Insight: Camila revealed that emotional factors—anxiety, fear of disappointment, desire for group harmony—are just as important as functional features in restaurant decision-making.

Design Implications

Together, Laura and Camila guided critical design decisions: the swipe interface addresses Laura's need for speed while the detailed restaurant profiles and collaborative filtering ease Camila's anxiety. The group matching system ensures both users feel heard—Laura gets efficiency, Camila gets reassurance that everyone agrees. This dual perspective created a product that's both fast and emotionally considerate.
