MatchFork

Transforming group dining decisions through collaborative swiping

The Problem

Many people face difficulties deciding where to go eat, especially in groups, due to the diversity of options, preferences, and dietary restrictions. This generates frustration, wastes time, and creates conflicts, affecting the overall dining experience.

The Context

In urban areas, the wide range of gastronomic offerings and diversity of tastes complicate restaurant selection. Currently, there are no efficient tools that facilitate group decision-making in a fast and enjoyable way.

The Solution

A digital solution that simplifies and streamlines decision-making about where to eat, considering individual and group needs to improve the collective experience. MatchFork combines personalized filters, group voting, and a playful swipe interface inspired by dating apps.

My Role

UX/UI Designer - User research, information architecture, wireframing, prototyping, and visual design

Research & Discovery

Understanding user needs through competitive analysis and user interviews

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed leading restaurant apps (TheFork, OpenTable, TripAdvisor) and found significant usability issues:

  • Search functionality scores: 2-4/10 - Users struggle to find restaurants efficiently
  • No group decision features - All apps focus on individual users
  • Information overload - TripAdvisor particularly suffers from cluttered interfaces
  • Poor mobile experience - Navigation is not optimized for quick decisions

User Personas

Through user interviews and research, we developed two key personas:

Laura Gómez - Proto Persona

Age: 25 | Occupation: Marketing Professional | Location: Buenos Aires

"We always take too long deciding where to eat; we need something that makes the process easier and faster."

Key Frustrations: Endless group discussions, lack of tools to centralize preferences, unintuitive existing apps

Goals: Simplify group decisions, discover new restaurants, save time

Camila Pérez - User Persona

Age: 20 | Occupation: Student | Location: Rosario

"I usually get anxious choosing a place to eat because I don't like it when the place doesn't meet my expectations."

Key Frustrations: Anxiety about disappointing others, scattered information across platforms, difficulty organizing options

Goals: Find quality places, have pleasant experiences, avoid group conflicts

Key Insights

  • Group dining decisions take an average of 25 minutes
  • 70% of users want group matching features
  • Anxiety and fear of disappointment are major emotional factors
  • Users need detailed information (reviews, photos, menus) to feel confident
  • The process should be fun, not stressful

Long-term Vision

For Users

MatchFork transforms dining from a source of stress into an opportunity for connection. By removing decision fatigue and ensuring everyone's voice is heard, groups spend less time debating and more time enjoying meals together.

For Restaurants

Restaurants benefit from qualified group bookings where all diners are genuinely interested, leading to better experiences and increased likelihood of repeat visits.

Market Impact

By solving a pain point no competitor addresses, MatchFork is positioned to capture frustrated users from existing platforms and establish itself as the go-to solution for social dining coordination.
